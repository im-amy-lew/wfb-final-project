Sacramento Weavers' and Spinners' Guild

Weaving by SWSG member Jan DeShera

The Sacramento Weavers' and Spinners' Guild is a nonprofit organization that encourages growth in the art of weaving, spinning, and dyeing via the interchange of ideas, research, and contact with experts in the field. SWSG presents members with the opportunity to meet and share through meetings, workshops, and lectures.

Guild meetings are currently held via Zoom in accordance with COVID-19 protocols.

(In-person meetings were usually held at the Shepard Garden and Art Center on the fourth Tuesday of each month at 10:00am, September through May, excluding December. Parking is free. Wheelchair accessible.)

The meetings begin with guild business, then proceed to educational programs. Bring your lunch; coffee and cookies are provided. After lunch, we return to presentations by featured speakers, which may include hands-on portions. Guests are welcome to meetings. A $3 guest fee is requested.

Membership is $35 a year payable in May.

Upcoming Workshops

Workshops are open to SWSG guild members. Due to COVID-19, in-person workshop attendance will require you to be masked.

Date: September 27, 2022

Time: Following the guild meeting.

Location: Shepard Garden and Arts Center, 3300 McKinley Blvd, Sacramento, CA; Zoom

Speaker: Stephenie Gaustad

Topic: Hemp From the Field to Fiber

This is a hands-on workshop where you will learn to apply stripping, hackling, and spinning techniques to the processing of hemp. Stephenie will review equipment that is used and any adjustments you need to make to your spinning wheel to work with hemp.

Students should bring: a functional spinning wheel or spindle, hand carders, oil for wheel as needed, and a niddy noddy for winding off yarn. A more detailed list will be sent out to participants.

Limit of 20 students.

Registration: $25
